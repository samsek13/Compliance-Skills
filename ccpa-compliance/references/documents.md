# CCPA Risk Assessment Template

## Legal Basis
§ 1798.185(a)(14)(B) — businesses whose processing presents significant risk to consumers' privacy
or security must submit a risk assessment to the California Privacy Protection Agency (CPPA).

## When Required (CPPA Guidance — High-Risk Factors)
- Large-scale processing of personal information
- Processing of sensitive personal information (§ 1798.140(ae))
- Use of automated decisionmaking technology, including profiling (§ 1798.185(a)(15))
- Processing of children's data (under 13 or 13–16 age verification)
- Cross-context behavioral advertising (§ 1798.140(k))
- New technologies with unknown privacy impacts
- Combining datasets from multiple sources
- Systematic monitoring of publicly accessible areas

# Service Provider Agreement Template

## Legal Basis
§ 1798.100(d), § 1798.140(ag) — written contract required for service providers.

---

## Required Contract Provisions (§ 1798.140(ag))

### 1. Purpose Specification
The personal information is disclosed by the Business only for the following limited and specified
business purposes:
- [LIST PURPOSES]

### 2. Privacy Protection Obligation
Service Provider agrees to:
- Provide the same level of privacy protection as required by CCPA
- Comply with all applicable CCPA obligations

### 3. Prohibited Activities (§ 1798.140(ag)(A–D))
Service Provider shall NOT:
- (A) Sell or share the personal information
- (B) Retain, use, or disclose PI for any purpose other than specified business purposes
- (C) Retain, use, or disclose PI outside the direct business relationship
- (D) Combine PI from Business with PI from other sources (except as permitted by regulation)

### 4. Right to Monitor Compliance (§ 1798.140(ag))
Business may monitor compliance through:
- Ongoing manual reviews
- Automated scans
- Regular assessments, audits, or testing at least once every 12 months

### 5. Notification of Non-Compliance
Service Provider shall notify Business if it determines it can no longer meet its CCPA obligations.

### 6. Remediation Rights
Business has the right, upon notice, to take reasonable and appropriate steps to stop and remediate
unauthorized use of PI.

---

## Subcontractor Engagement (§ 1798.140(ag)(2))
If Service Provider engages another person to assist in processing:
- Must notify Business of engagement
- Engagement must be pursuant to written contract binding subcontractor to same requirements

# Consumer Rights Request Procedure

## Legal Basis
§ 1798.130 — modalities for responding to verifiable consumer requests; § 1798.140(ak) — verification.

---

## Rights Summary

| Right | Section | When Applicable | Response Time |
|-------|---------|----------------|---------------|
| Know/Access | § 1798.110 | Any consumer (2x per 12 months) | 45 days (extendable) |
| Delete | § 1798.105 | Any consumer; exceptions apply | 45 days (extendable) |
| Correct | § 1798.106 | Inaccurate PI exists | 45 days (extendable) |
| Opt-Out (Sale/Share) | § 1798.135 | Business sells/shares PI | Immediate via link |
| Limit SPI Use | § 1798.121 | Business uses SPI beyond authorized purposes | Immediate via link |
| Non-Discrimination | § 1798.125 | Upon exercising any right | N/A (ongoing) |

---

## Request Handling Process

### 1. Designated Methods (§ 1798.140(n))
Accept requests via:
- [ ] Toll-free telephone number
- [ ] Website URL / web form
- [ ] Email address
- [ ] Other approved method

### 2. Verification (§ 1798.140(ak))
- Use commercially reasonable verification methods
- Match requestor to consumer about whom PI was collected
- For authorized agents: verify authorization (power of attorney, guardianship)

### 3. Response Timeline (§ 1798.130(b))
- Day 0: Request received and logged
- Day 45: Initial response deadline
- If extension needed: notify consumer before Day 45, cite reason (additional 45 days max)
- Total maximum: 90 days

### 4. Response Content
- **Access request**: Provide PI in readily usable format; disclose categories, sources, purposes,
  third parties (§ 1798.110, 1798.115)
- **Deletion request**: Delete and notify service providers/contractors; notify third parties
  who received sold/shared PI (§ 1798.105(c))
- **Correction request**: Use commercially reasonable efforts to correct (§ 1798.106(c))

### 5. Refusal & Appeal
- If request denied: inform consumer of reason and right to appeal
- Appeal process: [DESCRIBE]
- Consumer may file complaint with CPPA or Attorney General

### 6. No Charge (§ 1798.130(d))
- Requests are free of charge
- Exception: manifestly unfounded or excessive requests may charge reasonable fee

---

## Exemptions to Deletion (§ 1798.105(d))
- Complete transaction or perform contract
- Security and integrity
- Debugging errors
- Free speech
- Legal compliance
- Scientific research with informed consent
- Internal uses aligned with consumer expectations
- Legal obligation

# Opt-Out Preference Signal Specification

## Legal Basis
§ 1798.135(b), § 1798.185(a)(18) — technical specifications for opt-out preference signals.

---

## Signal Requirements (§ 1798.185(a)(18))

### Consumer-Friendly Design
- Clearly described and easy to use by average consumer
- No additional information required beyond what is necessary
- Free of defaults constraining or presupposing consumer intent
- No conflict with other commonly used privacy settings

### Required Choices (§ 1798.185(a)(18)(vi))
On the settings page/view:
1. **Global opt-out**: "Do Not Sell/Share My Personal Information" + limit sensitive PI use
2. **Limit sensitive PI**: "Limit the Use of My Sensitive Personal Information"
3. **Cross-context behavioral advertising**: "Do Not Sell/Do Not Share for Cross-Context Behavioral Advertising"

### Age Specification (§ 1798.185(a)(18)(B))
Signal must allow consumer/parent/guardian to specify:
- Consumer is less than 13 years of age
- Consumer is at least 13 and less than 16 years of age

---

## Business Response Requirements (§ 1798.185(a)(19))

### Prohibited Responses
Business shall NOT:
- Intentionally degrade functionality
- Charge a fee
- Make products/services not function properly
- Coerce consumer to opt-in
- Display intrusive popups or banners

### Permitted Opt-In Consent Link
- Not part of popup/banner that obscures intended page
- Does not require clicking to receive full functionality
- No dark patterns
- Applies only to the specific business
