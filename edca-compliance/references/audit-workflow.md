# EDCA Compliance Audit Workflow

This reference file contains the detailed step-by-step workflow for conducting an EDCA compliance audit. Read this when performing a full compliance assessment.

---

## Pre-Step — Read Source Materials & Plan Coverage

**MANDATORY**: Before beginning the audit:

1. **Read EDCA structure**: Grep for article numbers to understand the full scope
2. **Read Art. 1º** for scope/applicability → Read that article and parágrafo único
3. **Read Art. 2º** for definitions → Read relevant definitions for the product type
4. **Create Coverage Plan**: List all 41 articles and preliminarily mark applicability:
   - Articles clearly applicable → will analyze
   - Articles possibly applicable → will analyze and confirm
   - Articles likely N/A → will explicitly mark with reason
5. **For each topic area**, read the corresponding source article before making assessments

**This ensures every finding cites actual statutory text, not assumptions, and no article is skipped.**

---

## Step 1 — Determine Applicability (Art. 1º)

Read Art. 1º and its parágrafo único from the source file first. Then assess:

**Is the product/service within EDCA scope?**
- **Directed at children/adolescents**: Product/service specifically designed for this audience
- **Likely accessed by children/adolescents** (Art. 1º, parágrafo único):
  - I - Sufficient probability of use and attractiveness for children/adolescents
  - II - Considerable ease of access and use by children/adolescents
  - III - Significant risk to privacy, security, or biopsychosocial development

## Severity Guide

- 🔴 **High** = Direct violation risk, significant penalty exposure (Art. 35º sanctions)
- 🟡 **Medium** = Gap requiring remediation, potential compliance risk
- 🟢 **Low** = Best-practice improvement, minor enhancement

---

## Critical Reminders

1. **Never skip any article** - All 41 articles must appear in the Coverage Checklist
2. **Bilingual citations always** - Every Portuguese quote must have English translation
3. **Group by risk level** - Findings organized by severity, not by analysis sequence
4. **Global consistency** - Statistics in Executive Summary must match detailed findings
5. **Relocate on risk change** - When a finding's severity changes, move it to the correct section
